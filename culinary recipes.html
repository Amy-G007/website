Anna Potatoes

(T.t.=To Taste, A.N.=As Needed)

  • 1 1/2 lb - Russet or Mealy potatoes
  • T.t. - Salt & Pepper
  • A.N. - Clarified Butter

    • Preheat oven to 400 degrees, peel, wash, and trim potatoes, cut into 1/8 inch slices and reserve in a bowl. toss with salt and pepper. Do not wash or store in water
    Butter the inside of a heavy skillet, or mold if available. Select the most uniform shaped potatoes, start at the outside edge and work towards the center. Cover the bottom by over lapping potatoes in a spiral pattern.
    now assemble a spiral pattern border against the inside edge. Dot with a bit of clarified butter and season as layers move up. Fill the empty space with sliced potatoes, sprinkle clarified butter and season as you go. 5 tp 6 layers, place a final even laywer and baste with clarified butter.
    Cover skillet and heat for a moment on the stove top, bake for 30 - 40 minutes. To serve - unmold onto plate to drain and collect excess clarified butter. Serve immediately. Salt and Pepper to taste

Poached Salmon (hollandaise)

Yield: 4 Servings

  • 3 Qt - Water (2.8Ltr)
  • 1 lb - Carrots 1/4inch Slices
  • 1 lb - Onions 1/4inch Slices
  • 4 oz - Leeks 1/4inch Slices
  • 8 oz - Dry White Wine
  • 4 - Fresh Thyme Springs
  • 4 - Fresh Parsley Stems
  • 4 - Black Peppercorns
  • 1 - Bay Leaf
  • 4 - Salmon Fillets, skin removed, 4 oz each

    • Make Court-bauillion - Combine water, carrots, onions, leeks, wine, thyme, parsley, peppercorns, and bay leaf in a 8 quart pot. Bring to a boil over high heat, reduce to a simmer, cook for 20 minutes, skim as needed.
    Slowly, bring bouillon to gentle simmer, medium heat (5-10 minutes). When simmering, place fish one at a time in pot, regulate heat, keep at a gentle simmer until reached desired internal temperature. Salmon that is rare in the center is 115 degrees F (46 degrees C) medium cooked 125 degrees F(52 degrees C)
    As soon as it is done, transfer the pan to safe spot, carefully lift salmon out and on to a rack to drain, cover to keep warm. Serve immediatlely

Hollandaise

Yield: 4 Servings, 2 oz each

  • 1 oz - Cidar Vinegar
  • 1/8 tsp - Black Peppercorns
  • 1 oz - Water
  • 3 - Egg Yolks
  • 5 oz - Clarified Butter(warmed)
  • T.t. - Lemon Juice
  • T.t. - Salt & Pepper

    • Place vinegar and peppercorns in a small pan over medium heat, reduce until almost dry. 1-2 minutes.
    Add water and strain reduction, in a stainless steel bowl combine egg yolds and reduction and whisk until frothy.
    Place the bowl over a sauce pot with simmering water and whisk the egg mixture to the ribbon stage. Should have a light but firm consistancy and triple in size.
    Remove from heat and gradually add clarified butter, whisking constently until all butter emulsifies into egg yolds. Strain if desired.

Stuffed Tomato Duxelle

Yield: 4 servings

  • 2 oz - Clarified Butter
  • 1 oz - Onion, Minced
  • 12 oz - Mushrooms, minced fine, moisture free
  • 4 - Fresh Parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tblsp - Minced Garlic
  • 2 tblsp - Oil
  • 2 tblsp - Panko
  • T.t - Salt & Pepper
  • 4 - Roma Tomatoes

    • Preheat oven to 375 degrees F, Preheat meduim saute pan. Add clarified butter to the pan, heat it a little before adding the onion to sweat very briefly.
    Add the mushrooms and cook until brown and dry. Stirring often, 10-12 minutes, add 1 tblsp parsley and season with salt & pepper.
    Score, core, blanch, and peel tomatoes, cut off 1/4inch off the top of each tomato and scoop out the seeds in the center. Season with salt and pepper,
    Stuff tomatoes with mushroom mixture. Mix together garlic, panko, and oil, sprinkle on top, place top of tomato back on, and brush with olive oil and season to taste. Heat in oven until hot, 5-7 minutes.
